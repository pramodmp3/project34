Stackly Pharma

Driving Precision Medicine Through Technological Convergence

Our History

Stackly Pharma Labs was founded in **2015** with a singular goal: to challenge conventional drug discovery by integrating cutting-edge **bioinformatics and AI** into every stage of development. Born out of a research collective at MIT and Stanford, we recognized that the bottleneck in pharmaceutical R&D was not chemical synthesis, but the identification and validation of novel, high-potential targets.

Our initial success came in **2018** with the development of our proprietary **NeuroStack** screening platform, which dramatically accelerated the preclinical phase for neurodegenerative candidates. Today, we stand as a leader in precision oncology and gene therapy, committed to compliance, ethics, and sustainability in all global operations.

We believe in building better medicine, one breakthrough at a time, guided by data and driven by human need.

Our Vision

To pioneer the next generation of precision therapeutics, making previously untreatable diseases manageable, and redefining the global standard for pharmaceutical innovation and patient care.

Our Mission

  • **Accelerate Discovery:** Employing advanced AI and machine learning to cut the drug discovery timeline by 40%.
  • **Ensure Purity:** Maintaining the highest global cGMP standards in all manufacturing and compounding processes.
  • **Uphold Ethics:** Committing to transparent and ethical clinical trials and responsible data management.
  • **Foster Collaboration:** Partnering with global leaders in academia and biotech to amplify research impact.

Key Milestones

2015
Company Foundation & Seed Funding
Stackly Pharma Labs is officially established in the Boston Biotech corridor, securing initial funding to focus on computational chemistry.
2018
NeuroStack Platform Launch
Successful in-house development and deployment of NeuroStack, our proprietary AI target identification system for CNS disorders.

GMP Manufacturing Expansion
Opened a 50,000 sq ft, fully automated manufacturing facility compliant with current Good Manufacturing Practices (cGMP).

The lead compound in our immuno-oncology pipeline successfully progressed into multi-center Phase II clinical trials.

Published a groundbreaking paper detailing a novel, safer vector design for in-vivo gene editing, opening new avenues for rare disease treatment.
